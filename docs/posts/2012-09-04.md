<h3 style="text-align: left;">




















June 21 report</h3>
<br />
As part of the Bears Initiative and Lower Sproul Redevelopment, the OCF, like many other student groups in Eshleman, will be moved to a temporary location in Hearst Gym ("<i>the Grotto</i>") around August 2012. We hope to see you during the fall semester in Hearst Gym.<br />
<br />
We will do our best to ensure that the move proceeds smoothly, but please bear with us as we cope with the well-intentioned powers that be. There may be server downtime sometime between July and December 2012 (to be announced).<br />
<br />
The OCF will continue to offer its services moving forward, <i>including a computer lab</i>, although the lab space in Hearst Gym will be smaller.<br />

<h4 style="text-align: left;">




















Lab</h4>
<div style="text-align: left;">
We're planning to reopen the lab in Hearst Gym on <strike>the first day of fall semester, <b>Thursday, <span class="il">August</span> 16th</b></strike> <strike><b>Tuesday, August 21st</b></strike> <strike><b>Monday, August 27th</b></strike> <b>Tuesday, September 4th </b>(see below) with the same <a href="http://docs.ocf.berkeley.edu/wiki/Contacting_OCF_staff#When">hours</a> as before (<strike>dependent on the new building hours which we're working to address</strike> resolved, see below). Our tentative move date is <b>Saturday, August 11th</b>, and we don't have access to the space before [or after] then, so this doesn't give us a lot of time.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
The new lab space is significantly smaller. Currently we have 28 desktop computers; in Hearst Gym we'll have <b>16 desktop computers</b> and 2 printers. Considering that members (including OCF staff!) often had to wait for an available workstation as it is, this is problematic, but it's the only possible compromise (we were planning on five more but ASUC rejected them last week because of space constraints).<br />
<br />
In terms of creature comforts, there will be a single small couch (right now we have three large ones), possibly some comfy chairs (right now we have five), and no refrigerator (right now we have a refrigerator, microwave, and toaster).<br />

<h4 style="text-align: left;">




















Putting everything in perspective</h4>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Two years ago OCF completed its 15-month move from Heller Lounge in MLK 
to the larger "permanent resting space" in Eshleman, with excessive lab and server downtime 
(caused in part by <a href="http://archive.dailycal.org/article/103245/computer_lab_move_faces_several_delays">the discovery of asbestos and later a failed pump flooding the new lab</a>, then <a href="http://archive.dailycal.org/article/104301/computing_facility_re-opens_in_new_location_after_">a pipe burst dropping raw sewage</a>, then <a href="http://ocfblog.blogspot.com/2009/08/continuing-downtime.html">a network and later power outage</a> which was <a href="http://ocfblog.blogspot.com/2009/08/you-know-what-they-say-about-academia.html">prolonged</a>). Once again, we're moving, except this time our space is smaller, the timeframe is shorter, and budgets are tighter...</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
17 months of emails, phone calls, and meetings with the ASUC, Auxiliary, Capital Projects, architects, and planners: <b>we're frustrated</b>. Frustrated by the iterations of plans and people that come and go, leaving us to start all over again. So frustrated that the impending move is something to look forward to.<br />
<br />
But please <b>don't point fingers at anybody</b>: it's easy to blame the University or the ASUC. The reality is complicated and decisions are not easy. "They have a difficult job in that they are trying to <a href="http://justinsayarath.wordpress.com/2012/02/25/surge-project-update-lower-sproul-redevelopment-with-images-of-floor-plans/">cut a cupcake into a million little pieces</a>."<br />
<br />
Here are some kind words during our last move from our friends upstairs at the Daily Californian that mean so much to us:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<a href="http://clog.dailycal.org/2008/09/17/ocf-languishes-reopening-to-be-postponed-further/"><b>OCF Languishes, Reopening To Be Postponed Further</b></a><br />
by Danica Li, The Daily Clog (Sep 17, 2008) </blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Everyone loves the Open Computing Facility. It’s got a public refrigerator, microwave, <i>and</i> toaster, clever decorative tastes, free web space, and a central, lower Sproul location. 250 pages of free printing a semester ain’t something that’s easy to turn down, either. </blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Unceremoniously evicted from their sweet Heller Lounge digs at the end of last semester, the OCF has had to pack their things and haul ass to the basement of Eshleman Hall. The computer lab was scheduled to open by the beginning of the semester. So what’s holding things up? </blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Apparently, construction’s stalled because the OCF are waiting on funds from the ASUC. They also discovered a leak under the flooring in the lounge of the Publication Center, so OCF volunteers — bless their souls — have been scrambling to get that fixed as well. </blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
The target reopening date is still listed as mid to late September on the website. Godspeed, guys. You provide a top-notch public service, and we wish you the best of luck in hassling ASUC for funds.</blockquote>
Hopefully you'll still love us without the refrigerator, microwave, and toaster and with increasingly inconvenient locations (2nd fl MLK Heller Lounge, basement Eshleman, basement Hearst Gym).<br />
<br />
We've <i>still</i> got major leaks when it rains, undesirable guests (cockroaches and mice, not the computer kind, even in our spotless but comfy server room -- it's time to get a cat, see below). There are some things we won't miss. We'll miss our self-assembled tables and hardwood floors though.<br />
